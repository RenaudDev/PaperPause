## Essential Tools for the Aquatic Artist

To bring the depths of our shark collection to life, we believe the choice of medium is just as critical as the choice of color. The ocean is a world of shifting gradients and complex textures; therefore, the tools you select will dictate whether your shark appears as a sleek, silent hunter or a vibrant, stylized icon. At PaperPause, we curate our designs to respond beautifully to professional-grade supplies that offer a high **pigment load** for both precision and expressive flow.

When it's time to render the unique skin of a shark, we often find ourselves choosing between wax-based and oil-based colored pencils. For those seeking a painterly, saturated look, we recommend wax-based pencils like **Prismacolor Premier**. Their soft, buttery cores are ideal for **burnishing**—a technique where you apply heavy pressure to fill the **tooth of the paper** completely, resulting in a smooth, glass-like finish that mimics the slickness of a shark emerging from the surf. 

Conversely, for the intricate linework found in our more detailed compositions, we suggest oil-based pencils such as **Faber-Castell Polychromos**. These pencils possess a harder lead that holds a sharp point longer, making them superior for **scumbling**. By applying light, circular layers of different shades, you can build up the subtle, sandpaper-like texture of a shark’s dermal denticles without losing the clarity of the underlying design.

For artists who prefer bold, modern aesthetics, alcohol-based markers like Copic or Ohuhu offer unparalleled vibrancy. We love using these markers to lay down a streak-free base layer of deep oceanic blues or tropical teals. Because alcohol markers are translucent, they allow for sophisticated "under-painting." We often apply a marker base and then use colored pencils on top to add fine highlights, such as the glint of light on a treasure chest or the delicate veins in a jellyfish. At PaperPause, we encourage you to view your coloring kit as a professional toolkit; high-quality pigments provide the **lightfastness** and blendability required to transform a simple coloring page into a piece of fine art.

## Advanced Techniques for the Deep

To truly capture the majesty of an apex predator, we must move beyond flat color and embrace the subtle interplay of light and shadow. This technique is essential for depicting the "counter-shading" of a shark—the dark top and light belly—which provides their natural camouflage.

## Optimizing Paper and Printing

At PaperPause, we understand that the foundation of a masterpiece lies in the quality of the substrate. To truly honor the intricate details of our shark collection, we recommend using high-quality cardstock or heavy-weight mixed-media paper. For most coloring enthusiasts, a weight of at least 160 GSM (approx. 60lb) is the baseline, though we prefer 200 to 300 GSM (up to 110lb) to prevent any potential warping or bleed-through, especially when layering heavy pigments.

The texture, or **tooth of the paper**, dictates how your medium interacts with the surface. When we use **Prismacolor Premier** pencils, a slightly textured paper allows for superior pigment grip, enabling rich **burnishing** without premature wax bloom. For those who prefer the oil-based precision of **Faber-Castell Polychromos**, a smoother surface facilitates delicate **scumbling** and fine detail work around a shark’s dorsal fin.

When printing, we always suggest adjusting your settings to "High Quality" or "Best" and selecting the "Heavy Paper" or "Cardstock" media type in your printer dialogue. This ensures the ink bonds perfectly to the page without smudging. By selecting materials that can handle a high **pigment load**, we transform a simple printout into a professional-grade canvas, ensuring our artistic journey is as seamless as a shark gliding through the deep.
